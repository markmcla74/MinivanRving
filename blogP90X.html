P90X: 15 years later

I bought the P90X workout program in 2009. Back then, P90X was a revolutionary home workout program because it was so intense, and because Tony Horton, the creator of P90X, was such a great motivator. I loved these routines, and I still follow them today - 15 years later! (As a warning though: I have never performed more than three or four P90X workouts per week to avoid overtraining.) Over time, my exercise library grew, as I became curious about other home exercise programs. My favorite routines from my exercise library, and additional tips to stay fit are shown below:


Favorite Home Exercise Routines
P90X Core Synergistics, P90X Legs and Back
P90X2 Yoga
P90X3 Complex Upper, P90X3 Complex Lower, P90X3 The Warrior, P90X3 Accelerator, P90X3 Decelerator
Rushfit Total Body Strength and Conditioning
Scott Sonnon's Flow Fit
Steve Maxwell’s Spartan 300 Workout


Additional Tips to Stay Fit
Home workouts are all you need, but it’s still nice to mix things up, and to sometimes exercise at a gym.
When exercising at a gym, perform isometric exercises or bodyweight exercises when popular equipment is being used.
I also like to go to the gym to ride an exercise bike. I try to ride for about 20 minutes, alternating 1.5 minutes very difficult, 1.5 minutes moderate.
